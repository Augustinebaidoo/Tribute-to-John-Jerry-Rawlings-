Flight Lieutenant Jerry John Rawlings

The lengend who introduced democracy and ended Ghana's history of military rule...

A timeline into the life of Fli.Lt Rawlings:
  • 1947 - On June 22nd, Jerry John Rawlings was born to a Ghanaian mother and a Scottish father in Accra, Ghana.
  • 1966 - He was enrolled in Achimota School where he obtained his General Certificate of Education 'O' Level.
  • 1967 - He got recruited into the Ghana Air Force and got enlisted for the Flight Cadet Training at the Ghana Military Academy and Training School, Teshie, in Accra.
  • 1968 - He was posted to Takoradi in the Western Region for further training.
  • 1969 - In January, he passed out as a commissioned Pilot Officer, winning the coveted "Speed Bird Trophy" as the best cadet in flying and airmanship. He was commissioned a second lieutenant in the Ghanaian Air Force.
  • 1977 - He married his childhood sweetheart Nana Konadu Agyeman Rawlings.
  • 1978 - He earned the rank of Flight-Lieutenant and expert pilot, skilled in aerobatics. He joined the Elite Fourth Squadron of jet fighters.
  • 1979 - On 15th May, Flt.Lt. Rawlings attempt his first coup on the Military Head of state (Gen Fred Akuffo) which failed. He was arrested and appeared before a General Court Martial in Accra On May 28th, charged with leading a mutiny. He was scheduled for another court appearance on 4th June but he was sprung from custody by his army squard before the trials. He went on after the prison break to lead another coup revoltution (Armed Forces Revolutionary Council-AFRC) to overthrow Gen Fred Akuffo. He held the former leaders on trial on account of corruption and executed them. He went on to launched a "housekeeping exercise" to get rid of all those he perceived to be corrupt, a number of senior judges and military officers were executed. On 24th September, he allowed for a general election and handed over to the civilian government elected (Hilla Limann).
  • 1981 - December 31, after two years of weak civilian and deteriorating economy, Rawlings led Provisional National Defence Council (PNDC) to staged his third coup which overthrew Limann’s government.
  • 1983 - A great hungry hit Ghana as the economy continued to deteriorate, the situation got worse when million Ghanaians were expelled from the neighbouring country (Nigeria). He then adopted a conservative economic policies (Economic Recovery Programme - ERP) to revive Ghana’s economy. During this period through to 1987, He survived five coup attempt.
  • 1987 - Flt.Lt. Rawlings issued a government clampdown, in which opposition leaders were arrested and imprisoned.
  • 1990 - His conservative economic policies were succesful to stablizing the country's economy.
  • 1992 - September 14, He resigned from the Ghana Armed Forces. He called for democracy governance and held a public referendum to structure the 1992 constitution. He formed the National Democratic Congress (NDC) which contested and won the 1992 Presidential election.
  • 1993 - He was honoured a joint recipient of the1993 World Hunger Prize Award for transforming.
  • 1994 - He was awarded theClarence Martin Medalfor Support of Higher Education and theMartin Luther King Jr. Drum Major for Justice Award.He was also appointed the ECOWAS Chairman during the impasse of the first Liberian war.
  • 1996 - He was re-elected as the president of Ghana for another four years governance.
  • 2001 - His second term as president came to an end completed.
  • 2010 - In October, the African Union appointed Jerry John Rawlings as the AU High Representative for Somalia to “mobilize the continent and the international community to fully assume its responsibilities and contribute actively to the quest for peace, security and reconciliation in Somalia.”
  • 2020 - He went to rest forever in the bossom of the Almighty.
Jerry Rawlings' legacy is controversial and he divided opinion domestically and in the wider world. His detractors accused him of torture, corruption and worse. To his supporters, he brought order, security and prosperity to Ghana.
